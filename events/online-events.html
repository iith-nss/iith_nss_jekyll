World Environment Day 2024



Hello everyone,


World Environment Day is celebrated annually on June 5th to promote awareness and action for protecting our environment. The celebration of World Environment Day aims to encourage people to appreciate the planet's natural resources and work towards conserving them for future generations.


This year, we would like to conduct various online events to promote environmental awareness, encourage sustainable practices, and inspire action towards protecting our planet based on this year's theme, Ecosystem Restoration.


Events:

  1. Pledge taking
  2. Hand-Drawn Poster
  3. Tree Planting Photo/Video

Pledge Taking:


As part of our World Environment Day celebrations, we invite you to take a pledge to show your commitment to protecting and restoring our planet. By recording and sharing your pledge, you help inspire others to take action and join the movement for a sustainable future.


Instructions:

  1. Record a short video of at most 2 min of you taking the attached pledge. Drive link to pledge
  2. The video length should strictly be in the specified range.
  3. Shoot the video in good lighting so that you are visible and your voice is audible.
  4. Stand up on your feet, place your right hand on your heart and take the pledge above. Only then will your video entry be considered for evaluation.
  5. Compress the video file, upload it in .mp4 format to your drive, and share it with NSS for view access. Upload the drive link using the Google form.

Hand-Drawn Poster:


Express your creativity and commitment to the environment by participating in our hand-drawn poster event. Create a poster on the given topics to communicate the importance of ecosystem restoration visually.


Topics:

  • The Role of Trees in Ecosystem Restoration.
  • Visualizing the Future: A Restored Planet.

Instructions:

  1. Create a hand-drawn poster on any one of the given topics. We prefer you use an A4 sheet for your drawing to ensure uniformity.
  2. A maximum of 20 words can be used in the poster. Quotes can be used as long as they satisfy the word limit mentioned. Be simple and avoid complex quotes.
  3. A picture can be used from the Internet as a reference for the drawing. If used, please send the links to the image(s).
  4. The poster should contain the logos of NSS and IITH. You can add the IITH and NSS logos after scanning your drawing. It should not contain references to other entities, including student names.
  5. The poster you sent should meet minimum standards. Failing to meet the minimum standards means you will not be awarded any hours.
  6. Upload a scanned copy or a clear photograph of your artwork to your drive and share it with NSS with view access. Upload the file using the provided Google form.

Tree Planting Photo/Video:


Join us in making a tangible impact on the environment by planting a tree. Capture the moment with a photo or video to showcase your contribution to ecosystem restoration and encourage others to do the same.


Instructions:

  1. Plant a tree and document the process with a photo or a short video of 3-5 minutes.
  2. The video length should strictly be in the specified range.
  3. Shoot the video in good lighting and conditions. Ensure the photo/video is clear and the planting action is visible.
  4. Upload the photo/video to your drive and share it with NSS with view access. Upload the drive link using the provided Google form.

Please submit your entries by filling out this form.


Only one file can be uploaded under a question in the above form. Hence, if your entry is not a single file, please upload a zip file.


Only one entry per topic is allowed in each event. In case of multiple entries on a topic, hours will be given to the best one.


You can upload videos, photos and posters directly using the provided form. Make sure to give NSS access to every file you upload.


We will not consider entries mailed to NSS for evaluation. Please submit the entries only through the form.


The best entries will be uploaded on our Instagram handle.


Plagiarism is strictly not tolerated. Any case might lead to negative hours for NSS volunteers.
